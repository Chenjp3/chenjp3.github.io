Research Interests

My research interests focus on the progenitors, physical mechanisms, and observational properties of high-energy transient phenomena, including gamma-ray bursts (GRBs), fast X-ray transients, and tidal disruption events (TDEs).

By combining theoretical modeling, multi-wavelength data analysis, and hydrodynamic simulations, I study the dynamical evolution and radiation processes of transient sources to interpret their observational signatures and explore their underlying physical origins.

Since October 2025, I have been serving as an observation duty assistant for the Einstein Probe (EP) mission. Since November 2024, I have been an affiliated scientist of the SVOM (Space-based multi-band astronomical Variable Object Monitor) scientific team.
